Logan does Chekov tonight

THE CHERRY ORCHARD Act 4 by Anton Chekov

LOPAKHIN.

Eight roubles a bottle. [Pause] It's devilish cold here.

YASHA.

There are no fires to-day, we're going away. [Laughs]

LOPAKHIN.

What's the matter with you?

YASHA.

I'm just pleased.

LOPAKHIN.

It's October outside, but it's as sunny and as quiet as if it were summer. Good for building. [Looking at his watch and speaking through the door] Ladies and gentlemen, please remember that it's only forty-seven minutes till the train goes! You must go off to the station in twenty minutes. Hurry up.

[TROFIMOV, in an overcoat, comes in from the grounds.]

TROFIMOV.

I think it's time we went. The carriages are waiting. Where the devil are my goloshes? They're lost. [Through the door] Anya, I can't find my goloshes! I can't!
